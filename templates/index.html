What is this box?

This is beanbot, an RFID-based system for keeping track of your coffee in a modern, over-engineered way. Note that this is essentially an electronic honesty box - your card will not be modified by this system, and you will not be charged through college or otherwise. Payment must still be made by bank transfer.

How do I use it?

  1. Select the coffee you wish to mark on your record.
  2. Tap your camcard.
  3. That's it!

Registration

This box cannot read the contents of your RFID card. It is therefore necessary to associate your crsid with your card in a short registration step.

If you tap an unregistered card, you will be presented with the following screen:

Turn the knob to select 'Yes' from beneath, and click the knob to confirm. You will then see a page of CRSid's:

Simply turn the knob to select yours, and click to confirm.

If you make a mistake

Email db985 to re-associate your card with the correct CRSid.

If your CRSid is not shown

Email db985 to add your CRSid to the database. If you are impatient, you can instead Scroll to the special value OTHER,

and enter your crsid (or indeed any psuedonym you desire) by hand.

FAQ

Who is maintining this?

Diego Barberena, db985@cam.ac.uk

Will this break my camcard?

As far as I can tell, no. It has been used well over 1000 times by at least 30 different people, with no reported issues so far.

Can you read my college account / card data?

I read only your card's ID, all other data on camcards is encrypted. I cannot even read your CRSid directly, which is why you need to register.

Do I have to use my camcard?

Basically any 125kHz RFID card will work with this system.

Such cards can be provided for you if you would prefer not to use your camcard.

Do I have to use my real crsid?

Strictly speaking, no - however, using a psuedonym will lead to confusion for the accountants.

My camcard doesn't work

For reasons as yet unclear, there have been reports of camcards not being recognised by the reader. If this is the case, you can either

  1. Use a different RFID card you own,
  2. Buy a standard white RFID card (these are less than Â£1.00 each), or
  3. Ask Diego to find you a spare RFID card.
